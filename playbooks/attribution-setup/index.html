Attribution Models Explained

Before implementing attribution, you need to understand the models available and which one fits your business. Each model answers a different question about marketing effectiveness.

First-Touch Attribution

100% credit to the first interaction that brought a lead into your funnel.

First Touch = 100%

Last-Touch Attribution

100% credit to the final interaction before conversion.

Last Touch = 100%

Linear Attribution

Equal credit distributed across all touchpoints in the journey.

Each Touch = 100% / N

U-Shaped (Position-Based)

Heavy credit to first and last touch, with remaining credit split among middle touches.

First: 40% | Middle: 20% | Last: 40%

W-Shaped Attribution

Credit to three key moments: first touch, lead creation, and opportunity creation.

First: 30% | Lead: 30% | Opp: 30% | Other: 10%

Custom Weighted

Data-driven weights based on your historical conversion patterns.

Weights = Historical Analysis

When to Use Each Model

The "best" attribution model depends on what you're optimizing for and your sales cycle complexity:

ModelBest ForLimitations
First-Touch Understanding which channels create awareness and fill top-of-funnel Ignores nurture and sales influence; overvalues broad reach campaigns
Last-Touch Sales teams wanting to know what closes deals; short sales cycles Undervalues awareness and consideration; favors branded search
Linear Simple baseline model; organizations new to attribution Treats all touches equally even when they're not equal impact
U-Shaped Lead generation focus; marketing-qualified conversions Misses the sales handoff moment critical in B2B
W-Shaped B2B with defined funnel stages; complex buying committees Requires accurate lead/opp creation timestamps
Custom Mature marketing orgs with 12+ months of clean data Requires data science resources; can overfit to past patterns
B2B Recommendation: Start with W-shaped attribution. It captures the three moments that matter most in B2B: how leads discover you, what converts them to leads, and what creates sales opportunities. Evolve to custom weighting after collecting 12 months of conversion data.

42's Attribution Philosophy

Perfect attribution is a myth. Directional attribution is the goal. We've seen teams spend 6 months building "perfect" attribution models that still don't answer "should we spend more on LinkedIn?" Start simple: first-touch for acquisition channels, multi-touch for content influence.

The 42 Attribution Model

We run three models in parallel. Each answers a different question:

ModelWhat It CreditsUse It For
First-Touch 100% to the channel that created the lead Paid media budget decisions. "Where should we spend?"
Multi-Touch Linear Equal credit across all touchpoints Understanding the full journey. "What assists?"
Last-Touch 100% to the final conversion event Optimizing bottom-funnel. "What closes?"
Run all three. First-touch tells you where to spend. Multi-touch tells you what assists. Don't pick one -- the point is seeing the full picture and using the right lens for the right decision.

What Actually Matters for Attribution

Forget complex tools. These four things matter more than any platform:

1. UTMs on Every Link (No Exceptions)

Every paid ad, every email, every social post, every partner link. If it doesn't have UTMs, it doesn't exist in your attribution. Create a shared UTM builder and make it a policy, not a suggestion.

2. CRM Field for Original Source (Don't Overwrite)

The biggest mistake: letting last-touch overwrite first-touch. Create separate fields: Original Source (first touch, locked after set) and Converting Source (last touch, updates on each conversion). Never lose where they came from.

3. 90-Day Lookback Window

B2B sales cycles are long. A 7-day or 30-day attribution window misses most of your funnel. Set cookies for 90+ days. Your enterprise deals take months -- your attribution should reflect that.

4. Monthly Attribution Review (Not Real-Time Dashboards)

Real-time attribution dashboards are vanity metrics. B2B conversion data needs time to mature. Review attribution monthly with closed-won deals, not daily with incomplete data. One solid monthly review beats 30 noisy daily checks.

Skip the Attribution Tool Vendors

HubSpot/Salesforce attribution reports + UTMs + a spreadsheet beats a $50K attribution platform for most B2B teams.

We've worked with companies using Bizible, Full Circle, and other enterprise attribution tools. For 90% of B2B companies under $50M ARR, the complexity isn't worth it. Your CRM already has the data. Build reports, not integrations.

Technical Setup Requirements

Proper attribution requires consistent data capture across your entire marketing stack. Here's what you need to implement:

1. UTM Parameter Taxonomy

Create a standardized UTM naming convention that all team members follow:

ParameterPurposeExamples
utm_source The platform/site sending traffic linkedin, google, facebook, newsletter
utm_medium The marketing channel/type paid-social, cpc, email, organic-social
utm_campaign Campaign name or initiative q1-abm-fintech, webinar-march-2026
utm_content Ad/creative/asset variant carousel-v2, headline-test-b, cta-demo
utm_term Keyword (paid search) or audience b2b+attribution, enterprise-it-directors
Critical Rules:
  • Always use lowercase (linkedin not LinkedIn)
  • Use hyphens not underscores in values (paid-social not paid_social)
  • No spaces ever - they break URLs
  • Document everything in a shared UTM dictionary

2. CRM Field Setup

Create these custom fields in your CRM to capture attribution data:

Field NameTypePurpose
Original SourceTextFirst touch utm_source
Original MediumTextFirst touch utm_medium
Original CampaignTextFirst touch utm_campaign
Converting SourceTextLast touch before form fill
Converting MediumTextLast touch medium
Converting CampaignTextLast touch campaign
Self-Reported SourceDropdown"How did you hear about us?"
First Touch DateDateWhen first cookie was set

3. Tracking Pixel Implementation

Deploy these tracking mechanisms via Google Tag Manager:

  • First-party cookie: Set a 365-day cookie on first visit capturing UTM values
  • Session cookie: Track current session UTMs for last-touch attribution
  • Platform pixels: LinkedIn Insight Tag, Meta Pixel, Google Ads tag
  • CRM tracking: HubSpot or Salesforce tracking script
// Example: Capture and persist UTMs in first-party cookie
function captureUTMs() {
  const params = new URLSearchParams(window.location.search);
  const utmKeys = ['utm_source', 'utm_medium', 'utm_campaign',
                   'utm_content', 'utm_term'];

  let utmData = {};
  utmKeys.forEach(key => {
    if (params.get(key)) {
      utmData[key] = params.get(key).toLowerCase();
    }
  });

  // Only set if we have UTM data and no existing cookie
  if (Object.keys(utmData).length && !getCookie('attribution_first')) {
    setCookie('attribution_first', JSON.stringify(utmData), 365);
  }

  // Always update session/last-touch cookie
  if (Object.keys(utmData).length) {
    setCookie('attribution_session', JSON.stringify(utmData), 1);
  }
}

HubSpot Attribution Setup

HubSpot provides built-in attribution reporting, but requires proper configuration to be accurate:

Step 1: Enable Attribution Reporting

  1. Go to Reports > Attribution
  2. Enable "Contact Create Attribution" and "Deal Create Attribution"
  3. Select your default attribution model (start with Linear)

Step 2: Create Custom Attribution Properties

HubSpot's built-in fields capture some UTM data automatically, but create these custom properties for full control:

PropertyTypePopulated By
Attribution - Original SourceSingle-line textWorkflow on first form submission
Attribution - Original MediumSingle-line textWorkflow on first form submission
Attribution - Original CampaignSingle-line textWorkflow on first form submission
Attribution - Converting SourceSingle-line textMost recent form submission
Attribution - Self ReportedDropdownForm field: "How did you hear about us?"

Step 3: Form Field Mapping

Add hidden fields to your forms to capture UTM parameters:

  • Add hidden form fields for each UTM parameter
  • Use JavaScript to populate from URL or cookies
  • Map hidden fields to HubSpot contact properties
// HubSpot form ready callback
window.addEventListener('message', function(event) {
  if (event.data.type === 'hsFormCallback' &&
      event.data.eventName === 'onFormReady') {

    // Get UTM data from cookie
    const utmCookie = getCookie('attribution_first');
    if (utmCookie) {
      const utmData = JSON.parse(utmCookie);

      // Populate hidden fields
      const form = document.querySelector('form');
      if (form.querySelector('[name="utm_source"]')) {
        form.querySelector('[name="utm_source"]').value =
          utmData.utm_source || '';
      }
      // Repeat for other UTM fields
    }
  }
});

Step 4: Build Attribution Reports

Create these essential HubSpot reports:

  1. Contacts by Original Source: Bar chart showing first-touch distribution
  2. Deals by Original Source: Revenue attributed to first touch
  3. Source to Deal Conversion: Funnel showing conversion rates by channel
  4. Self-Reported vs Tracked: Compare "How did you hear" to UTM data

Salesforce Campaign Attribution Setup

Salesforce requires Campaign Member tracking and Campaign Influence configuration for multi-touch attribution:

Step 1: Enable Campaign Influence

  1. Setup > Campaign Influence > Enable Customizable Campaign Influence
  2. Create attribution models (First Touch, Last Touch, Linear)
  3. Set auto-association rules for campaigns

Step 2: Configure Campaign Hierarchy

Structure campaigns to enable roll-up reporting:

  • Parent Campaigns: Channel level (LinkedIn, Google Ads, Events)
  • Child Campaigns: Individual initiatives (Q1 ABM, Webinar Series)
  • Member Status: Define stages (Sent, Responded, Attended)

Step 3: Create Custom Fields on Campaign

FieldPurpose
ChannelPicklist: Paid Search, Paid Social, Email, Event, Organic
Campaign TypePicklist: Awareness, Consideration, Conversion
Budget AllocatedCurrency field for ROI calculation
UTM CampaignText field matching your UTM taxonomy

Step 4: Automate Campaign Member Creation

Use Flow or Process Builder to automatically add leads to campaigns:

  • When Lead Created with UTM_Campaign = X, add to Campaign X
  • When Form Submitted (webinar), add to webinar campaign
  • When Activity Logged (meeting), add to Sales Engagement campaign
Pro Tip: Use Salesforce Campaign Influence reports to see influenced pipeline and revenue. Navigate to Reports > Campaign Influence to build attribution dashboards that show which campaigns influenced which opportunities.

Common Attribution Mistakes

These errors invalidate attribution data and lead to wrong budget decisions:

1. Inconsistent UTM Naming

"LinkedIn" vs "linkedin" vs "li" vs "linked-in" fragments your data. Use a shared UTM builder and enforce naming conventions.

2. Missing First-Touch Data

Only capturing last-touch UTMs means you can't see what channels create demand. Implement first-party cookies that persist across sessions.

3. Ignoring Dark Funnel

40-60% of B2B discovery happens in places you can't track: podcasts, communities, word-of-mouth, LinkedIn DMs. Use self-reported attribution to capture this.

4. Not Tracking Offline Touchpoints

Sales calls, events, and conferences don't automatically appear in attribution. Log these as CRM activities and include them in your model.

5. Using Platform-Reported Data

LinkedIn, Google, and Meta all take credit for the same conversion. Use your CRM as source of truth, not platform dashboards.

6. Reporting on Clicks, Not Revenue

Channel A has 1000 clicks but $10K revenue. Channel B has 100 clicks but $100K revenue. Always tie attribution to pipeline and closed-won revenue.

The Biggest Mistake: Treating attribution as a one-time setup. Attribution requires ongoing maintenance: quarterly model review, regular data quality audits, and continuous UTM enforcement.

How to Validate Attribution Data

Run these audits monthly to ensure attribution data quality:

Audit 1: UTM Coverage Check

  1. Pull all leads created in last 30 days
  2. Calculate % with Original Source populated
  3. Target: >90% coverage. Investigate gaps.

Audit 2: Platform vs CRM Comparison

  1. Export conversions from LinkedIn/Google/Meta dashboards
  2. Compare to CRM leads attributed to those channels
  3. Variance >20% indicates tracking issues

Audit 3: Revenue Reconciliation

  1. Sum attributed revenue across all channels
  2. Compare to actual closed-won revenue
  3. Difference = unattributed/direct revenue

Audit 4: Self-Reported Quality

  1. Review "Other" responses in self-reported field
  2. Add frequently mentioned sources as options
  3. Flag inconsistencies (says Google, UTM says LinkedIn)
Data Quality Targets:
  • UTM coverage: >90% of non-direct leads
  • Platform variance: <20% difference from CRM
  • Revenue reconciliation: <10% unattributed
  • Self-reported completion: >70% on demo forms

Self-Reported Attribution: The "How Did You Hear About Us" Question

Technical attribution can't track dark social, podcasts, or word-of-mouth. Self-reported attribution fills this gap.

Why It Matters

Studies show 40-60% of B2B buyers discover vendors through channels that are invisible to tracking:

  • Colleague recommendation (no click, no UTM)
  • Podcast mention (listening, not clicking)
  • LinkedIn organic post (shared in DMs)
  • Community discussion (Slack, Discord, Reddit)
  • Industry event conversation (no digital trail)

Implementation Best Practices

PracticeWhy
Use dropdown + Other text fieldStructured data + flexibility for new channels
Put on high-intent forms onlyDemo request, Contact Us - not every newsletter signup
Make it optionalDon't block conversions for this data
Review monthlyAdd frequently mentioned sources as dropdown options

Recommended Dropdown Options

  • Google/Search engine
  • LinkedIn (ad or post)
  • Colleague/Friend referral
  • Podcast
  • Industry event/Conference
  • Newsletter/Email
  • Online community (Slack, Reddit, etc.)
  • Blog/Article
  • Review site (G2, Capterra)
  • Other (please specify)

Analyzing Self-Reported Data

  1. Compare to tracked: If 30% say "Podcast" but 0% tracked to podcast UTMs, you're missing podcast attribution
  2. Weight by revenue: Which self-reported sources produce highest-value deals?
  3. Segment by ICP: Enterprise customers often discover differently than SMB
Pro Tip: When self-reported and tracked data conflict, believe self-reported. If someone says "colleague referral" but has a LinkedIn UTM, they likely clicked a link their colleague shared - the colleague is the true source.
