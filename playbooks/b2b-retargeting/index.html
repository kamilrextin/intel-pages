42's B2B Retargeting Playbook

Retargeting is your highest-ROI paid channel. We consistently see 5-10x ROAS on retargeting vs 2-3x on prospecting. But most B2B teams do it wrong: they retarget everyone with the same generic ad. Segment by intent level and serve stage-appropriate content.

-- From managing $10M+ in retargeting spend

Why B2B Retargeting Is Different

Most retargeting advice comes from B2C ecommerce: abandoned cart emails, urgency pop-ups, discount codes. None of that works for B2B. Here's why:

B2C Retargeting

  • Single buyer decision
  • Days to purchase
  • Impulse-driven
  • Discount = conversion
  • Individual tracking

B2B Retargeting

  • Buying committee (3-10 people)
  • 3-12 month sales cycle
  • Education-driven
  • Value = conversion
  • Account-level tracking
The Core Difference: B2C retargeting tries to close the sale. B2B retargeting tries to advance the deal to the next stage while staying top-of-mind across a long evaluation period.

The B2B Retargeting Opportunity

Only 2-3% of website visitors convert on first visit. The other 97% leave, often never to return. But these visitors have already shown interest by finding your site. Retargeting keeps them engaged across the buying journey.

Companies that implement proper B2B retargeting see:

  • 2-3x higher conversion rates vs prospecting campaigns
  • 40-60% lower CPA for retargeting audiences
  • 22% increase in brand recall among target accounts
  • 70% of influenced pipeline touched retargeting ads

Audience Segmentation Framework

Not all website visitors are equal. Segment your retargeting audiences by intent level:

Segment Definition Intent Level Volume
Hot Prospects Pricing, demo, contact page visitors High ~5% of traffic
Product Explorers Product/solutions pages, 2+ page views Medium-High ~20% of traffic
Engaged Leads Known contacts (form fills) who returned Medium ~10% of traffic
Demo No-Shows Booked demo but didn't attend Medium ~15% of demos
Closed-Lost Opps Lost deals (timing/budget, not bad fit) Medium ~30% of opps
Blog/Resource Visitors Content consumers only Low ~50% of traffic
Pro Tip: Start with hot prospects and work down. Most B2B companies try to retarget everyone equally. Focus 50% of budget on the top two segments.

42's Segment Strategy

Here's how we segment retargeting audiences for our clients:

Hot (Pricing/Demo Page)
  • Serve demo CTA
  • Case studies
  • Social proof
Warm (Multi-Page/Blog)
  • Mid-funnel content
  • Comparison guides
  • ROI calculators
Cold (Single Page/Bounce)
  • Educational content
  • Brand awareness
  • Thought leadership
42's Rule: Don't retarget 1-page visitors with "Book a Demo." You'll burn budget and annoy people. Match your CTA to their demonstrated intent.

Platform Strategy: LinkedIn, Google, Meta

Each platform serves a different purpose in B2B retargeting. Use them together, not interchangeably.

LinkedIn

Professional Awareness

Best for: Known contacts, CRM audiences, account lists. Professional context = higher intent signals.

Google Display

Broad Reach

Best for: Website visitors, frequency across the web. Lowest CPM = highest coverage.

Meta

Cost-Efficient Frequency

Best for: Creative testing, video views, mobile reach. Lowest cost per engagement.

Recommended Budget Split

Platform % of Retargeting Budget Primary Use Case
LinkedIn 50% CRM audiences, known contacts, account lists
Google Display 30% Website visitors, broad frequency
Meta 20% Video retargeting, creative testing
Minimum Audience Sizes: LinkedIn requires 300 matched members. Google Display needs 100 users. Meta requires 100 users. Build audiences before you can serve ads.

42's Platform Priority Stack

  1. LinkedIn - Highest B2B match rate. Expensive but worth it for high ACV products ($50K+)
  2. Meta - Cheap reach, good for brand awareness. Best creative testing platform
  3. Google Display - Only with tight placement exclusions. Otherwise you're paying for MFA bot traffic

Skip Google Display unless you can exclude MFA sites. Otherwise you're paying for bot traffic.

Timing Windows: Hot, Warm, Nurture

Recency determines intent. Structure your retargeting by how recently someone engaged:

Window Recency Intent Level Messaging Focus CTA
Hot 0-7 days Highest Direct conversion Book demo, start trial
Warm 8-30 days Medium Social proof, case studies See results, talk to sales
Nurture 31-90 days Lower Thought leadership Read report, join webinar
Email only 90+ days Minimal Transition to email Newsletter, updates

Enterprise Exception

For enterprise sales (12+ month cycles, $100K+ ACV), extend nurture window to 180 days. These deals take longer, and staying visible matters more.

Implementation: Create separate campaigns per window. Use audience exclusions to prevent overlap. Hot excludes warm; warm excludes nurture.

Creative Strategy by Funnel Stage

Match your creative to where the audience is in their journey:

Hot Audience Creative (0-7 days)

  • Customer logos: "Trusted by [recognizable brand names]"
  • Specific benefits: "Save 10 hours/week on [pain point]"
  • Demo offers: "See it in action - 15 min demo"
  • Social proof stats: "2,500+ companies use [product]"

Warm Audience Creative (8-30 days)

  • Case studies: "[Company] achieved X result in Y time"
  • ROI calculators: "See your potential savings"
  • Comparison content: "[Product] vs [Competitor]"
  • Video testimonials: Customer success stories

Nurture Audience Creative (31-90 days)

  • Industry reports: "2026 [Industry] Benchmark Report"
  • Webinar invites: Expert panels, product updates
  • Thought leadership: "Why [trend] is changing [industry]"
  • Educational content: How-to guides, best practices
Creative Rotation: Rotate creative every 2-3 weeks. Even great ads fatigue. Plan 6-8 creative variants per quarter per audience segment.

Frequency Capping Best Practices

The difference between "staying top of mind" and "being annoying" is frequency control.

Platform Recommended Frequency Why
LinkedIn 3-5 impressions/week Professional context, higher attention per impression
Google Display 5-7 impressions/week Lower attention per impression, needs more frequency
Meta 7-10 impressions/week Scroll speed high, more impressions needed

Cross-Platform Total

If running all three platforms, total weekly frequency could reach 15-22 impressions. This is acceptable because:

  • Different contexts (work vs personal vs browsing)
  • Creative variation across platforms
  • Users don't consciously track cross-platform frequency
Fatigue Warning Signs: Monitor CTR by week. When CTR drops 50% from baseline, you've hit fatigue. Rotate creative or pause the audience temporarily.

The 42 Frequency Cap

7-10 impressions per user per week. More than that is stalking, not marketing. We've tested this across dozens of B2B accounts. Go above 10/week and you'll see brand sentiment turn negative in comment sections.

Budget Allocation for Retargeting

Most B2B companies under-invest in retargeting. Here's the framework:

Stage % of Paid Budget When to Use
Starting out 10% Under 10K monthly visitors, building audiences
Growing 15% 10-50K monthly visitors, audiences established
Mature 20% 50K+ visitors, robust audience pools

Never Exceed 20%

Beyond 20%, you hit diminishing returns. Retargeting audiences are inherently small. Over-investing leads to:

  • Frequency fatigue (showing ads too often)
  • Audience exhaustion (same people seeing ads for months)
  • Wasted spend on low-intent segments

Budget by Audience Segment

Segment % of Retargeting Budget
Hot prospects (pricing/demo visitors) 30%
Product explorers 25%
Engaged leads (known contacts) 20%
Demo no-shows 10%
Closed-lost opps 10%
Blog/resource visitors 5%

Exclusion Lists: Stop Wasting Budget

Exclusions are as important as inclusions. Remove these audiences from all retargeting:

  • Current customers: Upload customer email list as exclusion on all platforms
  • Competitors: Create list from competitor domain visitors (if trackable) or competitor employee emails
  • Job seekers: Exclude /careers page visitors and job-related traffic
  • Unqualified leads: Closed-lost marked as "bad fit" or leads that failed lead scoring
  • Recent converters: Exclude leads who converted in last 7-14 days to give sales clean follow-up window
Budget Savings: Proper exclusions typically save 15-25% of retargeting budget while improving conversion rates. It's free performance.

Measuring Retargeting ROI

Retargeting measurement is tricky because it often influences conversions without being the final click. Use these metrics:

Direct Metrics

Metric What It Measures Target
Click-through Rate (CTR) Ad relevance and creative quality 0.3-0.5% (LinkedIn), 0.1-0.2% (Display)
Cost per Click (CPC) Efficiency of ad spend Lower than prospecting CPC
Conversion Rate Direct click-to-conversion 2-3x higher than prospecting

Influence Metrics

Metric What It Measures How to Track
View-through Conversions Conversions after seeing (not clicking) ad Use 7-day window for B2B
Influenced Pipeline Deals that were exposed to retargeting Match ad exposure to CRM opps
Multi-touch Attribution Retargeting's role in deal journey Use linear or position-based models
Account-Level Tracking: For ABM, track account-level engagement (did anyone from this account see retargeting?) rather than just lead-level conversions.

Common Retargeting Mistakes

Avoid these pitfalls that kill retargeting performance:

  • Treating all visitors equally: A pricing page visitor is not the same as a blog reader. Segment by intent.
  • Running the same ad forever: Creative fatigue is real. Rotate every 2-3 weeks.
  • No frequency caps: Showing ads 50x/week creates brand damage, not brand awareness.
  • Retargeting customers: Showing "get a demo" ads to existing customers is awkward and wasteful.
  • Only measuring last-click: Retargeting often assists conversions. Use multi-touch attribution.
  • No timing windows: Treating 7-day visitors the same as 90-day visitors wastes budget.
  • Over-investing in retargeting: Beyond 20% of budget, returns diminish rapidly.

Implementation Checklist

Use this checklist to set up your B2B retargeting program:

Week 1: Foundation

  • Install LinkedIn Insight Tag, Google Ads tag, Meta Pixel
  • Create URL-based audiences for high-intent pages
  • Upload customer exclusion list to all platforms
  • Wait for audiences to build (need minimums)

Week 2: Campaign Setup

  • Create separate campaigns for hot, warm, nurture windows
  • Set frequency caps per platform
  • Build exclusion audiences between windows
  • Create 3-5 creative variants per audience

Week 3: Launch

  • Launch hot audience campaigns first
  • Set up conversion tracking for all platforms
  • Configure view-through conversion windows (7-day)

Ongoing: Optimization

  • Review frequency and CTR weekly
  • Rotate creative every 2-3 weeks
  • Update exclusion lists monthly (new customers, unqualified)
  • Report influenced pipeline quarterly
