MQL to SQL Conversion Rates

The MQL to SQL conversion rate measures how effectively marketing-qualified leads become sales-qualified. This is the most contentious metric in sales-marketing alignment.

Performance Level MQL to SQL Rate What It Indicates
Poor < 10% Lead scoring miscalibrated, ICP mismatch, or SDR capacity issues
Average 10 - 20% Room for improvement in qualification criteria
Good 20 - 30% Healthy alignment between marketing and sales
Excellent > 30% Strong ICP targeting, well-calibrated scoring

MQL to SQL by Lead Source

Warning: MQL to SQL rates above 40% may indicate your MQL threshold is too high, causing you to miss qualified leads. Balance quality with pipeline coverage.

SQL to Opportunity Conversion Rates

SQL to Opportunity measures the effectiveness of discovery calls and initial sales conversations. Low rates here typically indicate SDR qualification issues or poor discovery execution.

Performance Level SQL to Opp Rate What It Indicates
Poor < 25% SDR qualification too loose, or AEs not converting discoveries
Average 25 - 35% Standard performance, room to optimize handoff
Good 35 - 50% Strong SDR-AE alignment, quality discoveries
Excellent > 50% Excellent qualification, may indicate overly tight SQL criteria

Opportunity to Closed Won (Win Rates)

Win rate measures sales effectiveness once an opportunity is created. This varies significantly by deal size, complexity, and competitive landscape.

Performance Level Win Rate What It Indicates
Poor < 15% Pipeline quality issues, poor deal qualification, or competitive weakness
Average 15 - 22% Standard for competitive markets
Good 22 - 30% Strong qualification and sales execution
Excellent > 30% Excellent, but may indicate pipeline is too narrow
Pro Tip: Competitive win rates above 40% often signal you're not pursuing enough deals. Expand your pipeline coverage while maintaining quality through better MEDDIC/BANT qualification.

Conversion Rates by Company Size (Target Segment)

Selling to SMB vs. Enterprise requires different benchmarks. Larger deals mean lower conversion rates but higher contract values.

Segment MQL to SQL SQL to Opp Win Rate Avg Cycle
SMB (1-50 employees) 25-35% 45-60% 25-35% 14-30 days
Mid-Market (51-500 employees) 20-30% 35-50% 20-28% 45-90 days
Enterprise (501-5000 employees) 15-25% 28-42% 18-25% 90-180 days
Strategic (5000+ employees) 10-20% 20-35% 12-22% 180-365 days
42 Agency Insight: SMB deals close faster but churn faster. Enterprise deals take longer but have higher retention. The math often favors mid-market as the optimal balance of volume and economics.

Methodology

These benchmarks are compiled from multiple sources:

  • 42 Agency client data from 200+ B2B SaaS companies (2022-2026)

  • Peer benchmarking data from RevOps communities
  • CRM exports from HubSpot, Salesforce, and Pipedrive

Data represents primarily North American and European B2B companies with annual revenues from $1M to $500M ARR. Enterprise segments (5000+ employees) include Fortune 1000 data points.
