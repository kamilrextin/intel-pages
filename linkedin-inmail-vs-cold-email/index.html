LinkedIn InMail vs Cold Email

Which channel wins for B2B outreach? Response rates, costs, and when to use each

Updated April 2026 Based on 50K+ outreach campaigns

The Short Answer

LinkedIn InMail = Higher response rates (10-25%), but limited volume and higher cost per send. Best for enterprise accounts and executives.

Cold Email = Lower response rates (1-5%), but unlimited scale at low cost. Best for volume plays and mid-market.

The winning strategy? Use both. Start with email for reach, then use InMail as a pattern interrupt for high-value non-responders.

  • - Enterprise accounts ($100K+ ACV)
  • - Account list is small (<500)
  • - Emails bouncing or unresponsive
  • - Breaking through after email silence
  • - Referencing mutual connections

Use Cold Email When...

  • - Large prospect list (1,000+)
  • - SMB or mid-market targets
  • - Managers and directors (not execs)
  • - Need automation and sequences
  • - Budget constraints

Use Both (Multi-Channel)

The Optimal Multi-Channel Sequence

Combining InMail and email increases response rates by 2-3x. Here's the proven sequence:

1
EMAIL Day 1: Initial cold email

Short, personalized, single CTA. No attachments or multiple links.

2
EMAIL Day 3: Follow-up email

Reply to first email. Add value (case study, insight).

3
LINKEDIN Day 5: LinkedIn connection request

Send connection with personalized note (not pitch).

4
EMAIL Day 7: Third email touch

Different angle or social proof. Reference LinkedIn activity.

5
INMAIL Day 10: InMail (pattern interrupt)

For high-priority non-responders only. Different message than email.

6
EMAIL Day 14: Breakup email

"Last attempt" framing. Often gets highest response rate.

Pro tip: Reserve InMail for Tier 1 accounts only. Use email-only sequences for Tier 2-3. This maximizes InMail credits for where they matter most.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average response rate for LinkedIn InMail vs cold email?

LinkedIn InMail averages 10-25% response rates, while cold email typically sees 1-5% response rates. InMail's higher response rate is due to LinkedIn's professional context, verified profiles, and the implicit social proof of being on a professional network. However, cold email allows much higher volume, so total responses can be competitive.

Is LinkedIn InMail or cold email more cost-effective for B2B outreach?

Cold email is more cost-effective at scale. Email infrastructure costs $100-500/month for unlimited sends, while InMail credits cost $0.80-1.50 each with strict monthly limits (50-150 credits depending on your LinkedIn plan). However, when measuring cost-per-meeting, InMail often wins due to higher response rates, especially for enterprise targets.

Should I use both LinkedIn InMail and cold email in my outreach strategy?

Yes, a multi-channel approach combining InMail and email typically outperforms single-channel by 2-3x. Best practice: Start with email (higher volume), then use InMail as a pattern interrupt for non-responders after 2-3 email touches. Reserve InMail for high-value accounts where response rate matters more than cost.

What are the compliance requirements for cold email vs InMail?

Cold email must comply with CAN-SPAM (US), GDPR (EU), and CASL (Canada) - requiring clear identification, opt-out links, and legitimate interest basis. InMail operates within LinkedIn's platform, so LinkedIn handles baseline compliance, but you must still follow their User Agreement. Neither requires explicit opt-in for B2B prospecting, but GDPR requires legitimate interest documentation.

How many LinkedIn InMails can I send per month?

LinkedIn Sales Navigator Professional gives you 50 InMail credits/month, Team gives 50/month, and Enterprise gives up to 150/month. Credits roll over for 90 days. You also get credits back if someone responds within 90 days. In contrast, cold email has no platform-imposed limits - volume depends on your domain reputation and infrastructure.
