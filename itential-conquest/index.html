Ansible Tower / Red Hat Automation Platform

Prepared for Itential • February 2026

Why this matters: Ansible Tower is the most widely deployed network automation tool in enterprise — which means there's a large pool of users experiencing its limitations daily. We scanned G2, Capterra, UpGuard, and industry publications to surface the specific complaints users have. These pain points become your messaging angles for conquest campaigns targeting Ansible users on LinkedIn.

What Users Are Saying

Expensive Licensing
"Ansible Tower is expensive, especially for small and mid-sized businesses."
"Tower has a licensing fee, making it more suitable for enterprise environments with advanced requirements and support needs."

— Spacelift, Capterra Reviews

Steep Learning Curve
"Your engineers will need time to get up to speed with it."
"Mastering advanced features and best practices may require some learning—concepts such as Jinja templating, roles, and playbooks' intricacies requiring time for beginners."

— Spacelift, UpGuard

Limited Flexibility
"Ansible Tower is not very flexible, and many alternatives offer higher levels of customization."
"It can only help you with your Ansible workflows—preventing unified management of other infrastructure tools like Terraform or Kubernetes in one platform."

— Spacelift Analysis

UI & State Management Issues
"Only 85% of what can be done from the command line can be accomplished via the UI."
"Unlike competitors, Ansible doesn't maintain awareness of system dependencies. It simply executes tasks sequentially and stops upon completion or failure."

— UpGuard
