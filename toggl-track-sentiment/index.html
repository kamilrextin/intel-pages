Executive Summary

  • Toggl Track leads in agency sentiment with a net score of +0.45, praised for its clean UI, flexible tracking, and profitability reporting. Users report 15% increase in billable hours after adoption.
  • Harvest wins on invoicing but loses on pricing. Agencies love the end-to-end billing workflow, but costs escalate quickly for larger teams ($12-17.50/user/month).
  • ClickUp has mixed time tracking sentiment despite strong overall PM ratings (G2: 4.7). Native time tracking is "not impressive" and lacks invoicing - agencies often pair it with Toggl or Harvest.
  • Clockify wins the budget-conscious segment with unlimited free users, but agencies complain about mobile app issues and lack of approvals workflow.
  • Timely's AI tracking divides opinion - praised for capturing missed billable hours (1 in 5 go unrecorded), criticized for pricing ($11-28/user) and AI misclassification.

Key Agency Pain Points: Who Wins?

Billable Hours Capture

Studies show 1 in 5 billable hours go unrecorded with manual tracking. Automation can raise capture from 77% to 96%.

Winner: Timely (AI auto-capture) / Toggl (+15% billable hours)

Client Invoicing

Agencies need seamless time-to-invoice workflows. One-click invoicing from tracked hours is critical.

Winner: Harvest (native invoicing with Stripe/PayPal)

Retainer Management

Tracking hours against evolving budgets and rolling over unused time requires flexible tools.

Winner: Toggl Track (profitability tracking at client/project level)

Team Adoption

Low adoption kills ROI. Agencies report chasing 15% of staff for timesheets before switching tools.

Winner: Toggl Track ("people actually started tracking")

Budget-Friendly Scaling

Per-seat pricing adds up fast for agencies with contractors and freelancers.

Winner: Clockify (unlimited free users)

Accounting Integration

QuickBooks/Xero sync eliminates manual data entry and reduces invoice errors.

Winner: Toggl Track & Harvest (native integrations)

Clockify's mobile issues and lack of approvals frustrate agency ops managers. Toggl's adoption story ("people actually started tracking") resonates.

Lead with Simplicity + Value

Timely's AI is impressive but expensive and sometimes wrong. Toggl's manual approach is simple, accurate, and costs less.

"Accurate time tracking without AI guesswork - or AI pricing"

Messaging Guidance

Emphasize (Toggl Strengths)

  • 15% increase in billable hours reported by agencies
  • Profitability tracking at project, client, and team level
  • "When we switched, people actually started tracking"
  • Clean, intuitive UI that encourages daily use
  • 60-80 hours/month saved on timesheet management

Avoid Drawing Attention To

  • No native invoicing (requires QuickBooks/Xero export)
  • Mobile app sync issues mentioned in reviews
  • Limited visibility into task-level details for managers
  • No GPS/location tracking (if that's needed)
  • Pricing on paid plans can add up for large teams
