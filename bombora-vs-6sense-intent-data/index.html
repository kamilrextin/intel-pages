What is Intent Data and How It Works

The Concept

Intent data identifies companies actively researching topics related to your product or service, signaling they may be in-market to buy.

By tracking content consumption patterns, search behavior, and engagement signals across the web, intent data providers help you identify accounts before they fill out a form or contact sales.

How It Works

  • 1 Track content consumption across B2B websites
  • 2 Match activity to company via IP, cookies, or login
  • 3 Compare against baseline to detect "surge"
  • 4 Score and deliver signals to sales/marketing

Three Types of Intent Data

1st

First-Party Intent

Signals from your own website: page views, downloads, time on site. You own this data.

2nd

Second-Party Intent

Partner data from review sites (G2, TrustRadius), publishers, or event platforms.

3rd

Third-Party Intent

Aggregated data from publisher networks (Bombora) tracking content consumption at scale.

How to Evaluate Intent Data Quality

Key Evaluation Criteria

  • 1
    Match Rate

    What % of your target accounts show intent signals? Higher is better, but above 40% may indicate noise.

  • 2
    Conversion Lift

    Do accounts with intent signals convert at higher rates? Run an A/B test comparing intent vs. non-intent accounts.

  • 3
    Signal Freshness

    How quickly do signals appear? Real-time > Daily > Weekly. Stale signals lose value fast.

  • 4
    Topic Relevance

    Can you map topics to your actual product categories? Test with 5-10 core topics before committing.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • !
    High Volume, Low Conversion

    Lots of "intent" accounts but no lift in conversion = noisy data or wrong topics.

  • !
    All Accounts Show Intent

    If 80%+ of your TAM is "surging," the signal is meaningless.

  • !
    Opaque Methodology

    Can't explain where data comes from = can't evaluate quality.

  • !
    No Trial or Pilot

    Reputable vendors offer pilots. No pilot = no confidence in their data.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Bombora, 6sense, and G2 intent data? +

Bombora collects intent data through a publisher co-op of 5,000+ B2B websites, tracking content consumption at scale. 6sense aggregates multiple intent sources (including Bombora) with proprietary AI to predict buying stages. G2 captures high-intent buyer signals from software review pages and comparison searches. Bombora costs $30K-$60K/year standalone, 6sense costs $50K-$150K bundled with their platform, and G2 costs $20K-$50K/year.

Is Bombora or 6sense better for intent data? +

Bombora is better if you want standalone intent data to feed into your existing tools at a lower cost ($30K-$60K vs $50K-$150K). 6sense is better if you want an integrated ABM platform with predictive AI that combines Bombora data with other sources. Bombora has deeper topic taxonomy (10,000+ topics vs ~3,000 for 6sense). 6sense has stronger buying stage predictions. Most companies use Bombora through a platform like 6sense, Demandbase, or their CRM rather than standalone.

How accurate is G2 intent data compared to Bombora? +

G2 intent data is generally more accurate for bottom-of-funnel software purchase signals because it captures explicit buying behavior (viewing pricing pages, reading reviews, comparing products). Bombora is better for early-stage awareness signals across broader topics. G2 intent is limited to software/tech purchases, while Bombora covers all B2B topics. For SaaS companies, G2 intent often has higher conversion rates but lower volume than Bombora.

How much does Bombora cost? +

Bombora pricing for standalone Company Surge intent data typically ranges from $30,000 to $60,000 per year depending on the number of topics, accounts, and integrations. Enterprise packages with unlimited topics and dedicated support can exceed $80K/year. Many companies access Bombora data through platforms like 6sense, Demandbase, or RollWorks which include Bombora in their pricing ($50K-$150K/year). Bombora also offers Audience Solutions for advertising at variable CPM-based pricing.

What are the best use cases for each intent data provider? +

Bombora is best for ABM account prioritization, early-stage awareness detection, and feeding intent into existing CRM/MAP systems across any B2B category. 6sense is best for full-funnel ABM with predictive buying stages, anonymous visitor identification, and coordinated sales/marketing activation. G2 is best for SaaS companies wanting to capture high-intent software buyers actively researching solutions, competitive displacement campaigns, and review-based social proof integration.
