B2B Marketing Channel Selection Framework

How to choose between LinkedIn, Google, Meta, and ABM based on your ICP, ACV, budget, and timeline. A data-driven framework for channel prioritization.

Most B2B companies waste budget on the wrong channels. They spread spend thin across LinkedIn, Google, and Meta without understanding which channels fit their business model.

The right channel mix depends on four factors: who you're targeting (ICP definition), deal size (ACV), available budget, and timeline to results. This framework helps you prioritize.

The Core Principle

LinkedIn = Target WHO they are (title, seniority, company). Google = Target WHAT they're searching for (intent). Meta = Retarget those who've already engaged.

When to Prioritize LinkedIn

  • ICP is defined by job function: "VP of Engineering at Series B+ SaaS companies"
  • Buyers don't search for your category (new/emerging solutions)
  • Need to reach multiple stakeholders in buying committees
  • Running account-based marketing programs

When to Prioritize Google

  • Buyers actively search for solutions: "best CRM for startups"
  • Competitive category with established search demand
  • Need faster time-to-pipeline (capturing existing intent)
  • Lower ACV products that need volume

When to Use Meta

  • Retargeting website visitors and engaged contacts
  • Brand awareness at scale (lower CPM than LinkedIn)
  • B2B2C products where users can influence purchase
  • Complement to LinkedIn/Google, rarely primary channel

ACV-Based Channel Selection

Your average contract value determines which channels justify their cost structure. High-touch channels like LinkedIn make sense for high ACV; volume channels like Google work better for lower ACV.

The $100K ACV Rule

At $100K+ ACV, you can afford $1,000-2,000+ per opportunity. This justifies LinkedIn's higher CPMs ($80-150 vs Google's $20-50) because precision targeting reduces waste. Below $25K ACV, LinkedIn often doesn't math out unless your ICP is hyper-specific.

Minimum Budget Thresholds

Underfunding channels leads to poor data and wasted spend. Each channel has a minimum viable budget below which you won't generate meaningful results.

ABM Platforms $5,000-10,000/mo $15,000-30,000/mo $50,000+/mo
Why Minimums Matter

LinkedIn needs ~$80-150 CPM and 15-20 conversions per campaign for optimization. At $3K/month, you'll get ~30-40K impressions but only 3-5 conversions. That's not enough data for the algorithm or for your testing.

Rule of thumb: Fund fewer channels properly rather than spreading thin across many.

Implementation Checklist

Before You Launch
  • Define ICP in channel-specific terms (LinkedIn attributes, Google keywords)
  • Calculate maximum CAC based on ACV (typically 15-25% of first-year ACV)
  • Confirm minimum viable budget for chosen channels
  • Set up cross-channel attribution (UTMs + CRM tracking)
  • Define success metrics by channel (LinkedIn = pipeline influence, Google = direct conversions)
  • Build retargeting audiences before launching top-of-funnel
  • Align sales on lead quality expectations by source

Key Takeaways

  • LinkedIn = WHO. Target by job title, seniority, company attributes. Best for demand creation and ABM.
  • Google = INTENT. Target by search behavior. Best for capturing existing demand.
  • Meta = RETARGETING. Re-engage visitors and contacts. Rarely primary channel for B2B.
  • ACV determines economics. $100K+ ACV justifies LinkedIn's premium. <$25K needs volume channels.
  • Fund fewer channels properly. $8K on one channel beats $4K on two.
  • Match timeline to channels. Google for this quarter, LinkedIn for next quarter, Content for next year.
