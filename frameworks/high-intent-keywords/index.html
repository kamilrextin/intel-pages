High-Intent B2B Keyword Framework

Stop wasting budget on low-intent traffic. Learn to identify, score, and prioritize keywords that convert into pipeline.

The Core Problem

Most B2B paid search campaigns fail because they chase volume over intent. A keyword with 10,000 monthly searches and 1% conversion produces fewer leads than one with 500 searches and 15% conversion.

High-intent keywords cost more per click but deliver 3-5x better ROI. This framework teaches you to find them systematically.

42's High-Intent Keyword Strategy

Bottom-funnel first, always. Most B2B teams waste budget on top-funnel keywords like "what is [category]" when they should own "[competitor] alternative" and "[product] pricing." Capture demand before you create it.

- From managing $20M+ in B2B Google Ads spend

The 4 Intent Tiers

Not all keywords signal equal purchase intent. The hierarchy below shows how intent correlates with conversion probability.

40 Tier 1

Transactional Intent

Ready to buy, compare vendors, or request demos. Highest conversion rates (5-15%).

[product] pricing [product] demo buy [product] [product] quote
30 Tier 2

Commercial Investigation

Actively researching solutions, comparing options. Strong conversion (3-8%).

[product] vs [competitor] best [product] [product] reviews [product] alternative
15 Tier 3

Informational Intent

Learning about problems or solutions. Lower conversion (1-3%) but builds awareness.

what is [product] how to [solve problem] [product] guide [topic] best practices
10 Tier 4

Navigational Intent

Looking for specific brand or site. Useful for brand protection only.

[brand name] [brand] login [brand] support

Rule of thumb: Allocate 60-70% of paid search budget to Tier 1 and Tier 2 keywords.

The Competitor Keyword Play

Bidding on competitor names is table stakes for B2B Google Ads. "[Competitor] alternative" converts 2-3x better than generic category terms. If you're not running conquest campaigns, your competitors are capturing your in-market buyers.

42's Keyword Research Process

Here's the exact process we use to build high-intent keyword lists for B2B Google Ads campaigns. This takes 2-3 hours but sets up months of effective targeting.

1

Export competitor keywords from SEMrush/Ahrefs

Pull organic and paid keywords from your top 3-5 competitors. Focus on keywords they're actively bidding on - they've already done the testing for you.

2

Filter by high-intent modifiers

Keep only keywords containing: alternative vs pricing demo buy. Discard everything else for paid.

3

Check search volume (minimum 50/month for B2B)

B2B keywords have lower volume than B2C. 50 searches/month is enough if intent is high. Don't chase volume - chase buyers.

4

Estimate CPC (expect $15-50 for high-intent B2B)

High-intent B2B keywords are expensive. Plan for $15-50 CPC. If you're seeing $5 CPCs, you're probably targeting low-intent terms.

Pro tip: Start with 50-100 high-intent keywords, not 500 broad match terms. You can always expand after you've proven ROI on the core set.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a keyword high-intent for B2B?
A high-intent B2B keyword signals active purchase consideration. Key indicators include: transactional modifiers (pricing, demo, buy, quote), comparison intent (vs, alternative, compare to), vendor research (reviews, case studies), and specific solution needs (enterprise, for teams, integration with). High-intent keywords typically have lower volume but 3-5x higher conversion rates than informational keywords.
How do I prioritize keywords by intent?
Use the 4-tier intent hierarchy: Transactional (buy, demo, pricing) > Commercial (vs, reviews, best) > Informational (how to, what is, guide) > Navigational (brand name, login). Allocate 60-70% of paid search budget to Transactional and Commercial tiers. Score each keyword on intent (40%), funnel fit (25%), volume (20%), and competition (15%) for prioritization.
What are the best high-intent keyword patterns for B2B?
Top B2B high-intent patterns include: Comparison (X vs Y, X alternative, compare X), Pricing (X pricing, X cost, X quote), Action (X demo, X trial, buy X), Validation (X reviews, X case study), Integration (X for Salesforce, X HubSpot integration), and Enterprise (enterprise X, X for teams). These patterns consistently show 2-4x higher conversion rates than generic terms.
How do I build a B2B negative keyword strategy?
Build negative keyword lists in 5 categories: Job seekers (jobs, careers, salary, hiring), Students/DIY (free, tutorial, template, course), Wrong audience (consumer, personal, home), Competitors (competitor brand names unless running conquest), and Non-commercial (wiki, definition, history). Apply shared lists at account level, then refine at campaign level based on search term reports.
What conversion rates should I expect from high-intent keywords?
B2B high-intent keywords typically convert at 5-15% (click-to-lead), compared to 1-3% for informational keywords. Transactional terms like "demo" or "pricing" often exceed 10% conversion. Expect CPCs 2-3x higher for high-intent terms, but cost-per-lead is usually 40-60% lower due to superior conversion rates. ROI from high-intent keywords is typically 3-5x better than broad match campaigns.
