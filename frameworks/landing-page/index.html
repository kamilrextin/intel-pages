1 The 5-Second Test

The most important test for any landing page. Show your page to someone for exactly 5 seconds, then ask them three questions.

The 3 Questions

After 5 seconds, your visitor should be able to answer:

  1. What does this company do? - Clear value proposition
  2. Who is it for? - Target audience clarity
  3. What should I do next? - Obvious call to action

Passing Examples

  • "Pipeline CRM for sales teams that close faster"
  • "DevOps platform that cuts deploy time by 70%"
  • "HR software for teams scaling past 100"

Failing Examples

  • "Next-generation platform for modern teams"
  • "Empower your business with AI"
  • "Transform your workflow today"

2 Form Field Impact on Conversion

Each additional form field reduces your conversion rate by approximately 5%. The data is clear: shorter forms convert better.

Form Fields Use Case Expected Impact
1 field (Email only) Content downloads, newsletter Baseline
2-3 fields Gated content, webinar signup -5% to -15%
4-6 fields Demo requests, trials -15% to -30%
7+ fields Enterprise RFPs, complex sales -30% to -50%

The Solution: Progressive Profiling

Instead of asking for everything upfront, collect information over multiple touchpoints:

  • First touch: Email only
  • Second touch: Company name + role
  • Third touch: Company size + use case

Use enrichment tools (Clearbit, ZoomInfo) to auto-fill company data from email domains.

3 Above the Fold Checklist

Everything above the fold should serve one purpose: get the visitor to take action or scroll for more information.

Required Elements

Outcome-focused headline Describe the transformation, not the tool. "Close deals 40% faster" beats "AI-powered CRM."
Subheadline with proof Include a specific differentiator or proof point. "Used by 500+ B2B sales teams."
Primary CTA button One clear action with a contrasting color. Use action verbs: "Start Free Trial", "Get Demo."
One trust signal Logo bar, G2 badge, or specific metric. Choose the most impressive one.
Product or result visual Show the product in action or the outcome achieved. No stock photos.

4 Social Proof Placement

Where you place social proof matters as much as what you show. Use this hierarchy for maximum impact.

Social Proof Hierarchy

  1. Logo bar immediately below hero

    Reduces bounce rate by 20%. Show 5-8 recognizable companies from target industry.

  2. Specific metric near CTA

    "Join 2,500+ B2B marketers" or "Trusted by teams at Salesforce, HubSpot, Stripe."

  3. Case study snippets mid-page

    Overcome objections with specific outcomes: "40% faster time-to-close" or "$500K saved annually."

  4. G2/Capterra badges near form

    Third-party validation builds trust at the moment of conversion.

  5. Full testimonials with photos near bottom

    Include name, title, company, and photo. Video testimonials perform 25% better.

Match Social Proof to Audience

Enterprise prospects want to see enterprise logos. Startup prospects want to see startup logos. Segment your landing pages by ICP and customize social proof accordingly.

5 CTA Copy Formulas That Work

The right CTA copy can increase conversions by 20-30%. Use these proven formulas.

[Action] + My + [Asset]
Get My Free Audit | Download My Guide | Start My Trial
[Action] + [Benefit]
Start Closing More Deals | See How We Compare | Get Faster Pipeline
[Action] + [Timeframe]
Start Free 14-Day Trial | Book a 15-Min Demo | See Results in 5 Minutes

High-Converting CTAs

  • "Get My Free Audit"
  • "Start Free Trial - No Card Required"
  • "See How [Company] Did It"
  • "Book a 15-Minute Demo"

Weak CTAs to Avoid

  • "Submit"
  • "Learn More"
  • "Contact Us"
  • "Request Information"

6 Mobile-First Principles

40-60% of B2B traffic is mobile, but desktop converts 2x higher. Optimize both, but design mobile-first.

Mobile Optimization Checklist

Touch targets minimum 44x44px Buttons and links must be easily tappable
Fixed bottom CTA Keep primary action visible while scrolling
Single-column layout No horizontal scrolling or side-by-side content
Headline under 8 words Mobile screens need shorter, punchier copy
Mobile-friendly input types Use tel, email, number input types for appropriate keyboards
Reduce form fields on mobile Consider mobile-specific offers: "Schedule a call" vs. long form

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good conversion rate for B2B landing pages? +
The average B2B landing page conversion rate is 2.5-5%. Top-performing B2B SaaS landing pages achieve 8-12% conversion rates. Enterprise software with longer sales cycles typically sees 1-3%. Conversion rate benchmarks vary by industry: Legal Tech 3-6%, Healthcare Tech 2-4%, FinTech 2-5%, DevOps/Engineering 1-3%.
How many form fields should a B2B landing page have? +
Each additional form field reduces conversion rate by approximately 5%. For top-of-funnel offers (content downloads), use 1-3 fields. For demo requests, 4-6 fields is acceptable but expect 15-25% lower conversion. For enterprise solutions, progressive profiling across multiple touchpoints performs better than long forms upfront.
What content should be above the fold on a B2B landing page? +
Above the fold must include: (1) Clear headline stating the outcome or transformation, (2) Subheadline with specific proof point or differentiator, (3) Primary CTA button with action-oriented copy, (4) One trust signal (logo bar, G2 badge, or specific metric), (5) Visual showing the product or result.
How do I optimize a B2B landing page for mobile? +
Mobile optimization essentials: Touch targets minimum 44x44px, CTA button fixed at bottom of viewport, single-column layout, headline under 8 words, mobile-friendly input types, reduce form fields on mobile. 40-60% of B2B traffic is now mobile, but conversion rates are 50% lower on mobile - optimize accordingly.
Where should social proof be placed on a B2B landing page? +
Social proof placement hierarchy: (1) Logo bar immediately below hero, (2) Specific metric near CTA button, (3) Case study snippets mid-page, (4) G2/Capterra badges near form, (5) Customer quotes with photos near bottom. Match social proof to your target audience - enterprise logos for enterprise prospects.
