The 42 Lead Scoring Model

Most lead scoring is broken. Teams score downloads and webinar attendance, then wonder why MQLs don't convert. Real lead scoring starts with ICP fit, not engagement. We've rebuilt lead scoring for 50+ companies using this framework.

The 42 Two-Axis Model

Lead scoring is a two-dimensional problem. Most companies only solve for one.

X

Fit Score (0-100)

Does this person match your ICP?

  • Company size (employee count, revenue)
  • Industry vertical
  • Tech stack (integrations, existing tools)
  • Job title / seniority level
Y

Engagement Score (0-100)

Are they showing buying signals?

  • High-intent page visits (pricing, demo, features)
  • Multiple sessions / return visits
  • Demo or sales requests
  • Content engagement depth

Fit score is gating. Engagement score is timing.

42's MQL Definition

Forget complex matrices. Here's the only formula you need:

MQL

Fit Score >= 60 AND Engagement Score >= 40

High-fit lead showing meaningful buying signals

NEVER

Fit Score < 40 = Never MQL

Regardless of engagement. No exceptions.

"A director at a 10-person company downloading 50 whitepapers is not an MQL."

Stop wasting sales time on leads that will never close. Fit score is gating. Period.

Score Decay

Old engagement doesn't mean current intent. Here's how we handle it:

Engagement Scores

Decay 10% per week of inactivity.

A demo request from 8 weeks ago is worth 20% of its original value.

Fit Scores

Never decay. Only update when data changes.

Company size doesn't change because they haven't visited your site.

Run decay calculations weekly via workflow automation. Reset decay clock when new high-intent action occurs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is lead scoring in B2B?

Lead scoring ranks leads by conversion likelihood. Effective B2B scoring combines ICP fit (firmographic match) and engagement (behavioral intent signals). The 42 Model uses a 100-point scale split evenly between these dimensions.

How do I build a lead scoring model?

Five steps: (1) Define ICP fit criteria from closed-won analysis, (2) Map engagement signals weighted by intent, (3) Implement score decay, (4) Set MQL threshold for 15-25 qualified leads per rep per week, (5) Calibrate against historical conversion data.

What is a good MQL score threshold?

A good threshold produces 15-25 leads per rep per week with 20%+ lead-to-opportunity conversion. Most B2B companies use 60-70 points on a 100-point scale. Require minimum ICP fit score to prevent poor-fit leads from qualifying on engagement alone.
